# SpecStudio.net Marketing Evaluation

### Where the marketing value breaks down

**1. No social proof whatsoever.** No client names, testimonials, anonymized outcomes, or results. The AIM case study link is a promising exception — but the other projects show no evidence of impact. A visitor with real budget has no anchor for trust.

**2. The CTA is passive.** "Start a Conversation" → `hello@specstudio.net` is a dead end. Cold-emailing a studio with no context on engagement model, timeline, or cost creates friction that kills conversions. You need either a short intake form or a direct calendar link.

**3. No pricing signal or engagement model.** "We help founders and technical teams" — doing what, exactly, for how long, and at what rough scale? Visitors self-select away when there's nothing to anchor expectations. Even a "typical engagement" description helps.

**4. Studio Notes are trapped.** Three genuinely good essays are buried in the homepage scroll with no way to subscribe, share, or follow new ones. That's a wasted content engine. A proper blog with RSS and a newsletter would compound authority over time and create inbound SEO.

**5. The GitHub link goes to `ConsciousnessResearch`.** This is a significant brand mismatch. For a studio selling architectural discipline and professional clarity, linking to a personal-project GitHub org undercuts the positioning immediately. Either create a SpecStudio GitHub org or remove the link.

**6. ICP is under-defined.** "Founders and technical teams" is everyone and no one. Who is the *ideal* client — pre-seed founder with a napkin idea? A technical team rebuilding a core system? The messaging would sharpen considerably by naming the situation more precisely.

**7. The project portfolio reads as exploratory, not commercial.** Jyotiṣa Aspectarian and Zhēn Yì are genuinely interesting, but they signal a practitioner's personal interests more than a studio's commercial track record. That's fine for a certain audience, but may create hesitation for mainstream B2B clients.

------

### Suggested improvements, prioritized

**Highest leverage:**

- Replace the email CTA with a short intake form (3–4 questions: project type, timeline, budget range) or a calendar link for a free 30-minute discovery call
- Add one or two case studies with real outcomes — even anonymized ("a Series A fintech team needed to rebuild their core data model before scaling…")
- Create a `SpecStudio` GitHub organization and migrate or link relevant repos there

**Medium leverage:**

- Spin the Studio Notes into a standalone blog with RSS; add a low-friction newsletter subscribe ("One essay per month. No noise.")
- Add a brief "how an engagement works" section — even 3 steps: Specify → Design → Build already exists, but attach it to a client journey, not just a service list
- Add outcome language: what does a client *leave with*? A specification document? A working MVP? A technical roadmap?

**Longer term:**

- Build a LinkedIn presence for SpecStudio (distinct from personal) and use it to distribute Studio Notes
- For the Jyotiṣa Aspectarian and Zhēn Yì — consider a waitlist or early-access email capture; these projects have a natural niche audience worth cultivating separately

------

Overall: SpecStudio has excellent *signal* — the philosophy is coherent, the copy is disciplined, the taste is evident. The gap is in *conversion infrastructure*. Right now it reads as a beautiful artifact rather than an active business development tool. The fixes are mostly structural, not requiring any change to the voice or positioning, which is genuinely strong.
